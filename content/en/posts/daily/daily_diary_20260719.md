*Written July 19, 2026*

Do you know what day today is?

It's the auspicious day when the second brain's first search after saving finally succeeded.

I leaned on AI a lot for the design, but the features and structure I needed are exactly as I proposed them.

Of course, it doesn't run the way I wanted yet, and there's still plenty that doesn't work properly — but I think it's tremendously encouraging.

Because, of course, **zero and one are different.**

---

There was no special reason behind my first attempt to build my own PKM, a second brain.

It just looked cool. That was all.

But as I studied, I started to get quite greedy. There I was, digging and digging with the thought, *'I'll get this system finished one way or another.'*

Even with my eyes dry and gritty, I'd review the plans in the brief spare moments between commutes, and when I couldn't follow something I'd ask the AI to explain it simply.

The instruction I gave the AI most often while building this project was probably this:

*'Explain it in a way that's easy to understand.'*

I clearly understood it yesterday, I definitely gave the instruction yesterday — and yet when it suddenly comes back at me with function names and variable names, my head starts to hurt.

The truth is, I knew nothing about things like RAG; it was only when I got to know the second brain this time that I learned RAG from the concept up.

And not just RAG. I had no idea how to use the various DBs, or how python commands are structured either, so this time I learned at least the outer shell of it all.

Maybe it was because I knew nothing that I found the courage — just reading the plans was fun.

Even while running in the morning, I'd catch myself turning it over in my head: *'Since it runs on this structure, for this to hold up...'*

Each time, it made me laugh. Because I felt that, even though I'm a developer, I'm someone who feels neither the aptitude for it nor the joy of development.

This project probably won't make me money, nor will it get my abilities recognized.

But this project has already come to mean quite a lot to me.
